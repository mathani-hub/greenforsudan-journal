Green For Sudan: Youth Science Communication Journal

Welcome! Your Voice Matters.

Open Call for Submissions!

The Green For Sudan Science Communication Journal is thrilled to announce we are open for contributions! We invite Sudanese youth, researchers, and science enthusiasts across the globe to share their insights and help build a sustainable, scientifically informed future for our nation.

As Sudan's first youth science communication journal, we believe in the power of local voices to highlight challenges, showcase innovation, and inspire action.

Key Areas of Focus:

Climate Change Clean & Affordable Energy Clean Water & Sanitation

We welcome articles from all scientific disciplines and other fields of study. Contributions can address any of the UN Sustainable Development Goals, as long as they align with the journal's mission and scope.

Read Submission Guidelines

A Platform for Sudan's Youth

We are delighted by your interest in contributing to Sudan's first youth platform dedicated to empowering young voices in science communication. This guide provides all necessary information to prepare and submit your compelling article, interview, or perspective. We aim to make complex scientific topics accessible, engaging, and highly relevant to the immediate reality of Sudanese communities.

Our Aims & Scope: What We Publish

The Green For Sudan Journal is committed to bridging the knowledge gap in critical areas impacting Sudan's sustainable development: climate change, water security, and energy efficiency. We seek engaging, evidence-based content that:

  • Translates scientific concepts into accessible language for a broad audience.
  • Highlights local challenges, innovations, and solutions within Sudan.
  • Empowers and educates our core audience: Sudanese youth and communities.
  • Fosters critical thinking and encourages action toward a sustainable future.

Who We Publish For (Our Audience):

Our primary audience consists of Sudanese youth, students, community leaders, and the general public interested in science, innovation, and sustainable development. We encourage writers to write clearly, concisely, and engagingly, avoiding overly technical jargon where possible, or explaining it in detail when necessary. Imagine you are explaining a scientific idea to an interested friend or family member.

General Submission Requirements

Originality

All submissions must be original work not previously published or under consideration elsewhere. We do not accept content entirely generated by AI without substantial human review and explicit declaration.

Language

All articles must be written in clear, concise, and grammatically correct English. (We may open to other languages later).

Plagiarism

We have a strict zero-tolerance policy for plagiarism. All sources must be properly cited.

Tone

Maintain a respectful, informative, and inspiring tone. Contributions should be constructive and evidence-based.

Author Bio

A short biography (50-100 words) highlighting your background and passion for science communication must be included.

Structure

Use clear headings and subheadings to break up text. Keep paragraphs concise for improved readability.

Article Types & Guidelines

We accept the following article types. Click each title to expand the detailed guidelines and see what we are looking for.

Perspective / Point of View

What it is: A well-researched, evidence-backed argument or informed opinion piece on a scientific, policy, or societal challenge relevant to Sudan. It presents the author's viewpoint, supported by facts and analysis.

Purpose: To stimulate critical thinking, encourage debate, and offer new insights or potential solutions from the perspective of Sudanese youth.

Key Elements: Strong thesis statement, brief background context, evidence-based arguments, potential counter-arguments addressed, and a clear call to thought/action.

Target Word Count: [600-1000 words]

Tone: Opinionated (evidence-based), respectful, analytical, persuasive.

Download Sample: [Perspective_Sample.pdf]
Explainer / Digest Article

What it is: A concise, clear article that breaks down a specific scientific concept, new research finding, or technology into digestible information for a non-specialist audience.

Purpose: To demystify science, promote scientific literacy, and provide foundational understanding of key topics relevant to our pillars.

Key Elements: Clear title, simple introduction defining the concept, gradual breakdown of ideas, use of analogies/metaphors, Sudan context linkage, and encouragement of simple visual aids.

Target Word Count: [1000-2000 words]

Tone: Clear, direct, educational, patient.

Download Sample: [Explainer_Sample.pdf]
Interview

What it is: A Q&A format presenting a conversation with a relevant expert, innovator, or community member about their work, insights, or experiences related to science and sustainability in Sudan.

Purpose: To bring diverse voices and direct expertise to our readers, offering personal insights and breaking down complex topics through dialogue.

Key Elements: Brief introduction of the interviewee, formatted questions and answers, natural flow and clarity. The author must secure the interviewee's permission for publication.

Target Word Count: [1000-2000 words]

Tone: Conversational, informative, respectful.

Download Sample: [Interview_Sample.pdf]
Case Study

What it is: A detailed examination of a specific real-world project, initiative, or problem-solving effort related to climate change, water security, or energy efficiency in Sudan. Highlights lessons learned and replicability.

Purpose: To showcase practical applications of scientific principles, inspire action, and provide concrete examples of sustainable development efforts.

Key Elements: Problem statement, intervention/solution, brief methodology, results/outcomes, lessons learned, and future recommendations/implications.

Target Word Count: [1000-2000 words]

Tone: Factual, analytical, practical, inspiring.

Download Sample: [Case_Study_Sample.pdf]
Feature Article

What it is: An in-depth, narrative-driven exploration of a scientific topic, discovery, or issue relevant to Sudan. Feature articles often tell a story, incorporate compelling examples, and go beyond surface-level information.

Purpose: To engage readers with a comprehensive, yet accessible, understanding of an important scientific topic or innovation.

Key Elements: Catchy title, intriguing introduction, narrative flow, context and background, examples and anecdotes, expert quotes (optional), and clear conclusion.

Target Word Count: [2000-3000 words]

Tone: Engaging, informative, sometimes inspiring, authoritative yet approachable.

Download Sample: [Feature_Article_Sample.pdf]

Citations and References

Accurate citation is essential for scholarly integrity and helps our readers explore the evidence behind your claims. We use the IEEE style for references.

Why Citation Matters:

  • Credibility: Shows your work is grounded in reliable information and research.
  • Transparency: Allows readers to verify information and delve deeper into topics.
  • Respect: Gives proper credit to the original creators of ideas and data.
  • Learning: Helps you organize your research and understand the scientific landscape.

How to Submit Your Article

  1. 1

    Prepare Your Abstract

    Draft a summary of your proposed article (maximum 250 words) in English.

  2. 2

    Author Biography

    Include a short biography (50-100 words) either in a separate file or within the submission form.

  3. 3

    Note on Images

    DO NOT submit images at this stage. If your abstract is accepted, you will be requested to send the full manuscript and all high-resolution images later.

  4. 4

    Fill Out the Submission Form

    Click the button below to navigate to our secure online submission portal (Google Form).

Images and Figures Guidelines (For Full Manuscript)

If your article includes images, figures, or illustrations (at the later stage):

  • Separate Files: Indicate the insertion point in the text (e.g., `[Insert Figure 1 Here]`), but upload images separately when submitting the full manuscript.
  • Copyright & Licensing: Ensure you have the right to use all images. Provide credit source and licensing information for non-original work.
  • Captions: Provide a clear, descriptive caption for each figure, numbered sequentially (e.g., 'Figure 1: Installation of solar panels...').

The Two-Phase Review Process

Phase 1: Abstract Review. Your abstract and author details will undergo an initial review by our editorial team. We aim to get back to you within one week with a decision.

Phase 2: Full Manuscript Invitation. If your abstract is accepted, you will receive specific instructions and a formal invitation to submit the full manuscript. You will then have 2 to 3 weeks from the date of acceptance to submit the complete Word document, images, and references.
